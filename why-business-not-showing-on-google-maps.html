Why isn't my business showing up on Google Maps?

The short answer

Google won't show a business on the map until it's confident the business is real, it knows exactly what you do, and other people are vouching for you. When you don't show up, it's usually because one of those three things is missing — not because Google has it out for you.

The usual culprits

  • Your Google Business Profile isn't verified. Unverified profiles have almost no visibility. Verification is a postcard, phone, or video step a lot of contractors skip.
  • Your profile is thin. No description, no service area, no photos, blank services — Google has less to work with, so you rank lower.
  • Wrong primary category. Listed as "Contractor" instead of "Painter" or "Insulation contractor"? You won't show up for the searches that matter.
  • Too few reviews. Reviews are one of the strongest map signals. Two reviews against a competitor's thirty is a losing hand.
  • Address or service-area issues. P.O. boxes and virtual offices often get filtered. Google prefers a real address or a clearly set service area.
  • Your name, address and phone don't match across your website, Facebook, and directories. Inconsistency makes Google less sure you're one real business.
  • Duplicate listings. Two profiles for the same business confuse Google and split your ranking.

The catch on timing

Even after you fix everything, the map doesn't move overnight. Local rankings compound — usually 3 to 6 months before you see real change. The businesses above you started months or years ago. The best time to plant a tree was yesterday; the next best is today.
