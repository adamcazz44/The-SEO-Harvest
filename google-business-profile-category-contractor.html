Why the category matters so much

When someone searches "painter near me," Google mostly shows businesses listed under the matching category. If yours is set to something generic like "contractor" or "construction company," you're telling Google you're a bit of everything — so it shows you for almost nothing. The category is how Google decides which searches you even qualify for, and it's easy to have it set wrong without ever realizing.

The mistake I see most

Far and away the most common one: a specialist listed under a generic, catch-all category. It feels accurate, but it's the equivalent of hanging a sign that just says "Business." It's also the kind of thing that can quietly get reset or mis-suggested over time, so it's not a set-it-and-forget-it deal.

What good looks like

Your listing should point squarely at your main trade, with the other things you do supporting it — not competing with it, and not padded out with categories you don't really serve.
