Picking the right Google category for your trade.

Your Google Business Profile category is one of the quietest, most powerful settings you've got — it tells Google which searches to show you for. Get it wrong and you can be invisible for the exact work you do. I was in construction for 22 years before I learned how much this one setting matters. Here's how to get it right.

Why the category matters so much

When someone searches "painter near me," Google mostly shows businesses whose primary category is Painter. If yours is set to the generic "Contractor" or "Construction company," you're telling Google you're a bit of everything — so it shows you for almost nothing. The category is how Google decides which searches you even qualify for.

How to get it right

  • Set your primary category to your main trade. Be specific: "Painter," "Roofing contractor," "Insulation contractor" — not "Contractor." This is the big one.
  • Add secondary categories for what else you do. A roofer who also does gutters can add a gutter category — but keep the primary one your bread and butter.
  • Don't stuff categories you don't actually do. Irrelevant categories confuse Google and can hurt more than help.
  • Match how customers describe the work, not industry jargon — pick the category a customer would search, not what's on your business license.
  • Re-check it after any profile edit or Google update; categories sometimes get reset or wrongly suggested.

The mistake I see most

Far and away the most common one: a specialist listed under the generic "Contractor." It feels accurate, but it's the SEO equivalent of hanging a sign that just says "Business." Switch it to your actual trade and you often see movement faster than from almost any other single fix.

How to change it

In your Google Business Profile, open Edit profile, find the Business category section, and set your primary category to your trade — then add any real secondary ones. Save, and give Google a little time to re-index. It's free and takes about five minutes.

Common questions

What's the difference between primary and secondary categories?

Your primary category is your main trade and carries the most ranking weight. Secondary categories cover other real services you offer. Choose the primary one carefully.

Can the wrong category really keep me from showing up?

Yes — it's one of the most common reasons a contractor is invisible for searches they should win. The wrong category tells Google to show you for the wrong things.

How many categories should I use?

One accurate primary, plus only the secondary categories you genuinely provide. More isn't better; relevant is better.
