Why reviews matter so much

Reviews do two jobs at once. They're one of the strongest signals Google uses to decide who shows up first on the map, and they're the first thing a customer reads before deciding who to call. A contractor with a wall of recent reviews beats an identical one with a handful — both in the rankings and in the customer's gut.

What a strong review profile looks like

It's not just a high star rating. It's:

  • enough reviews to look established
  • a steady stream of new ones so it never looks stale
  • thoughtful responses to the good and the bad

That combination tells Google you're active and tells a customer you're dependable. Getting there — and keeping it going month after month without leaning on your customers or cutting corners — is the part that takes a real system rather than a one-time push.

The one thing that backfires

Don't buy reviews or post fake ones. Google catches it and can pull your listing, which is far worse than having fewer reviews. Real reviews from real jobs are the only ones that hold up — and the only ones worth having.

Common questions

How many reviews do I need?

There's no magic number — the goal is to be in the same ballpark as the contractors ranking above you, and to keep new ones coming in steadily so you don't look stale.

Do reviews really affect my ranking?

Yes — how many you have, your rating, and how recent they are all feed into where you land on the map. It's one of the biggest levers you've got.

Is it against the rules to ask customers for reviews?

Asking happy customers is fine. Paying for reviews or posting fake ones is what breaks Google's rules and risks your listing.
