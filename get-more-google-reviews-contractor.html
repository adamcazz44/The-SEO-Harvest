How contractors get more Google reviews.

Reviews are one of the biggest reasons one contractor outranks another on Google — and the good news is they're free and fully in your control. I was in construction for 22 years before I learned how this works. Here's the honest way to get more of them.

Why reviews matter so much

Reviews do two jobs at once. They're one of the strongest signals Google uses to decide who shows up first on the map — and they're the first thing a customer reads before deciding who to call. A contractor with forty real reviews beats an identical one with three, both in the rankings and in the customer's gut.

You don't need hundreds. Steady, real reviews that keep coming in beat a big pile that stopped two years ago.

How to actually get them

  • Just ask — every time. Most happy customers will leave one; they just forget. Ask at the moment the job's done and they're happy.
  • Make it one tap. Text them your Google review link instead of saying "look us up." Every extra step loses people.
  • Ask the same day. A review request the evening of the job lands far better than one a week later.
  • Reply to every review — good or bad. It shows you're active, and Google notices activity.
  • Answer a bad one calmly. A fair, professional reply to a complaint earns more trust than a wall of perfect five stars.

What not to do

Don't buy reviews or post fake ones — Google catches it and can suspend your profile, which is far worse than having fewer reviews. Don't offer to pay for them either. Real reviews from real jobs are the only ones that hold up and the only ones worth having.

Common questions

How many Google reviews do I need?

There's no magic number — the goal is to be in the same ballpark as the contractors ranking above you, and to keep new ones coming in steadily.

Is it against the rules to ask for reviews?

No. Asking is fine and encouraged. Paying for reviews or posting fake ones is what breaks Google's rules.

Do reviews really help me rank?

Yes — review count, rating, and how recent they are all feed into where you land on the map.
